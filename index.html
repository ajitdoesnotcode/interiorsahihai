OUR STORY — 01

The reason this exists

It started with a broken dream and a bad suthar.

AhmedabadWhere it happened
₹3,00,000Lost to false trust
1 HomeMy first. My dream.
2 EMIsRunning simultaneously
₹3L
Gone. Just like that.
Wasted on a local contractor who promised the world, delivered rubble, and disappeared without a word. No accountability. No refund. No recourse. Just a gutted apartment and a lesson nobody should have to learn this way.
01 — THE DREAM

I had worked years for this. My first home — not just an apartment, but the place I had imagined every corner of, every wall, every feeling of walking in at the end of the day and thinking: yes, this is mine.

EMIs were running. I was still paying rent, living in a borrowed space while my dream space was being built. Two costs, one salary, and a heart full of excitement. I trusted a local suthar — a furniture contractor — with everything.

"He had references. He had confidence. He had all the right words. What he didn't have was integrity."

02 — THE BETRAYAL

The work was shoddy. Finishes that crumbled. Measurements that were off. Materials that were clearly not what was promised. And when I raised my concerns? He went silent. Then he disappeared. Three lakhs. Gone.

There was no platform to check his past work properly. No verified reviews. No portfolio I could trace to real projects. Just word of mouth and hope — the two most unreliable currencies when it comes to the one place you call home.

I had to start over. Find a real interior designer. Redo half the work. Spend again, while the EMI clock kept ticking and the rent kept going out. The anger wasn't just about the money — it was about vulnerability. About handing someone your most personal space and being betrayed.

At some point I stopped being angry and started asking a different question.
Why does this keep happening to people? Why is there no trusted place to find, verify, and hire an interior designer in a city like Ahmedabad — where thousands of new homes are being built every year, where people are spending their life savings, where the stakes couldn't be higher — and yet the process is still just hope and referrals and crossed fingers?

03 — THE DECISION

I'm not a startup guy who spotted a market gap on a spreadsheet. I'm someone who got burned — badly — and decided no one else should feel what I felt. The helplessness. The grief of watching your dream apartment turn into a construction disaster with no one to call.

Interiorsahihai is what I needed back then. A place where every designer is verified, every review is real, every portfolio is traced to actual projects, and every customer can make their decision with confidence — not fear.

A marketplace built not on ambition, but on a scar that became a solution.

"Your home is not just a space.
It's who you are.
It deserves better than hope."
