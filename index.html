Gone. Just like that.

I was paying two EMIs simultaneously — one for the home I was living in, one for the home I was building. The contractor disappeared. The work was half done. The money was gone. And I had no way to know who to trust next.

The Dream

Every Indian who buys their first home knows the feeling. The keys. The empty rooms. The imagination running wild — what goes here, what colour, what light. You've saved for this. You've planned for this. This is the home your family will grow in.

In Ahmedabad, like most Indian cities, the next step is usually a phone call to someone your neighbour knows, or a name from a WhatsApp group, or a contractor who shows up with confidence and a portfolio on his phone that may or may not be his work.

There was no way to verify. No portfolio that could be traced. No reviews from real people. Just faith — and faith is not a system.

The Betrayal

I lost ₹3 lakh to someone I trusted because I had no other option. Not because I was careless. Because the system gave me no better option. When it happened, I started asking around. It wasn't just me. Almost everyone I knew had a story — a contractor who vanished, work done badly, budgets doubled, timelines tripled.

This is a ₹1.8 lakh crore industry in India. And the most important decision — who actually does the work — was being made on pure gut feel and borrowed trust.

The Decision

Interiorsahihai exists because no one should have to go through what I went through. Not in their first home. Not in any home. We built the platform I needed — one where designers are verified, portfolios are real, reviews are honest, and quotes are transparent. Where you can compare, decide, and move forward with clarity.

We started in Ahmedabad. We're building across India. Because the problem isn't local — it's everywhere. And so is the dream of a home that finally feels like you.

Because your home is not just a space.
It's who you are.

— @misterphilosopher, Founder · Interiorsahihai
